Exploring Coastal Resilience, Flood Risk Assessment, and Infrastructure Safety

Closure Criteria for Coastal Roadways Under Flooding Conditions

Published

This research develops a comprehensive framework for assessing flood impacts on transportation systems and establishing road closure criteria for coastal roads. By integrating Delft3D-FM hydrodynamic modeling with empirical road closure data, geospatial analysis, and vehicle stability assessments, the study evaluates flood hazards under current and future climate scenarios including sea level rise projections.

Key Contributions:

  • Integrated hydrodynamic modeling with empirical closure data
  • Developed vehicle-specific closure criteria based on depth and velocity
  • Applied Monte Carlo sampling and SVM classification for hazard assessment
  • Assessed infrastructure resilience under sea level rise scenarios (2050 & 2100)

Gulf Coast Extreme Water Level Threshold Prediction via Deep Learning

2024 - Present | Ongoing

This project employs advanced deep learning techniques to predict extreme water level thresholds along the Gulf Coast. The research aims to improve early warning systems and enhance coastal resilience planning by developing accurate predictive models for storm surge and compound flooding events.

Key Contributions:

  • Development of deep learning models for water level prediction
  • Integration of historical water level data and meteorological forcing
  • Analysis of extreme value statistics for threshold identification
  • Application to Gulf Coast vulnerable regions
Presentation (ICCE 2026)

Collaborators: Dr. Hamed Moftakhari, Dr. William Sweet, Dr. Jayantha Obeysekera

Estimating Fine Resolution Local Sea Level Scaling Factors Using Ensemble Deep Learning

2024 - Present | Ongoing

This research focuses on developing high-resolution local sea level scaling factors using ensemble deep learning approaches. The project addresses the challenge of downscaling global sea level rise projections to local scales, enabling better coastal planning and adaptation strategies.

Key Contributions:

  • Ensemble deep learning framework for sea level prediction
  • Fine-resolution spatial analysis of sea level variability
  • Integration of tide gauge data and satellite altimetry
  • Development of location-specific scaling factors

Collaborators: Dr. Hamed Moftakhari, Dr. William Sweet, Dr. Jayantha Obeysekera

Bridging Return Period Gaps in Flood Inundation Mapping Using Hybrid Deep Learning

2024 - Present | Ongoing

This innovative project combines physics-based modeling with deep learning to create comprehensive flood inundation maps across multiple return periods. The hybrid approach addresses computational limitations of traditional methods while maintaining physical consistency.

Key Contributions:

  • Hybrid modeling framework combining physics-based and ML approaches
  • Generation of flood maps for multiple return periods
  • Computational efficiency improvements over traditional methods
  • Validation against observed flood events

Numerical Study of Skirted Foundations for Offshore Wind Turbines

2019 - 2022 | Completed

Master's thesis research investigating the behavior of skirted foundations for offshore and onland wind turbines on sand under lateral dynamic loading. The study provides insights into foundation design optimization for renewable energy infrastructure.

Key Contributions:

  • Numerical modeling of skirted foundation behavior under dynamic loading
  • Analysis of foundation-soil interaction for wind turbine structures
  • Review of existing numerical studies and future research directions
  • Design recommendations for offshore wind turbine foundations
